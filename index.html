Einar Viðar Guðmundsson Thoroddsen is a graphic artist, printmaker, and illustrator from Iceland, currently based in The Hague. He approaches projects with curiosity, always searching for the unexpected. His work primarily focuses on printmaking—especially RISO and screen printing—but he also dabbles in editorial design and occasionally creates logos and identities.

At KABK, he searched for and found an equilibrium between humor and seriousness, evident in both his practice and daily life. He draws inspiration from his surroundings, people’s mannerisms, and the overlooked details of everyday life. His approach is experimental, delving headfirst into the making part of the process and valuing it just as much as the final outcome—even the mistakes, as that is where things get interesting.
